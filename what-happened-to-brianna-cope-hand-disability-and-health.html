    Feelings of being different marked Brianna’s early years, but she channeled her pain into overcoming obstacles.

    Although she once posed with open hands in childhood photos, the cruelty of elementary school led her to close her hands, avoiding questions about her deformity.

    High school offered some relief as her surfing talent earned her respect, but the bullying scars remained.

    Instead of succumbing to negativity, Brianna embraced surfing, discovering her hand could be an advantage.

    She found she paddled faster without fingers, making it her “superpower.” Surfing became her passion and a haven where she could excel without judgment.
