    Enchanting Has Been Making Music Since Age 18

    Music wasn’t something the now rising rapper was into all her growing-up days like it wasn’t something she would say was a calling from the onset. In an interview with Essence, Enchanting revealed she went through a phase of doing absolutely everything to find her passion in music.

    She, however, took her love for the art to its next level after she turned eighteen. After graduating from high school, she made the studio her second home, and in no time, she recorded “a little mixtape” titled Love & Drugs.

    Although she sounded like “a little baby” on that tape (Enchanting admitted to that), that mixtape launched her into her music career and was well received by her fans This kept her pushing to keep recording.

    In 2020, she was signed to 1017 records and is currently the sole woman signed to 1017 Records. She was featured in K Shiday on Gucci Mane’s compilation tape; Gucci Mane Presents So Icy Summer. Enchanting credits J. White Did It (Dallas’ superproducer)  for making the connection between herself and Atlanta rap icon Gucci Mane possible.

    She has since evolved and taken on R&B as well. Speaking on taking the R&B Genre, Enchanting told Essence that she has been into the genre as long as she has been into music and that she only started rapping when I signed to Gucci.

    She has an EP on Apple Music titled Enchanted. She has several other singles, Eps, and videos being streamed on the app, including her latest release, P Valley: Season 2
