    The Foundations were a British soul band formed in 1967. The group consisted of seven members, including lead vocalist Clem Curtis and guitarist Alan Warner.

    Their sound was heavily influenced by Motown and other American soul music, and they had several hit singles in the late 1960s, including “Baby Now That I’ve Found You” and “Build Me Up Buttercup”.

    The Foundations were a British soul/R&B group that formed in London in 1967.     Who was the lead singer for the Foundations?

    The lead singer for the Foundations was Clem Curtis. He was born in Trinidad in 1949 and moved to England with his family when he was a child. Curtis was a charismatic performer with a powerful voice. He left the Foundations in 1970, but he continued to record and perform solo. He died in 2017 at the age of 67.
